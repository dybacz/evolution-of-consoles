Generation I

1967 - 1975 - Birth of an Industry

It all begins in 1967 with the devleopment of a bulky rectangular wooden box with two attached controllers. Known as "Brown Box" the first working prototype games console - this box was conceived by Ralph H.Baer - The father of Video Games.

1972 - Magnavox Odyssey

1975-1977 Atari Pong Arcade machine so popular it was released as a home console. Improvements to the odyssey lead to the release of not one but two improved/updated versions of the console named The Magavox Odyssey 100 and Odyssey 200 (not the last team we see the release of updated/improved consoles - running theme)
